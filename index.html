01 — About

A quality analyst's instinct, pointed at AI.

I spent more than a decade making work measurable — building quality-assurance frameworks, scoring outputs against multi-point criteria, and turning loose processes into repeatable systems. Most recently I've run operations for C-suite leaders, where the same discipline keeps governance, decisions, and follow-through on track.

That habit transfers cleanly to AI. The hard part of working with generative models isn't generating — it's judging: deciding whether an output is accurate, complete, on-instruction, and safe to use. That's evaluation, and it's what I've always done. I use AI tools daily and treat every output as a draft to be reviewed, not a result to be trusted.

The samples below are how I think on paper: the rubrics, prompts, workflows, and checks I build to make AI-assisted work reliable.

Sample 01
AI Output Evaluation Rubric
Quality & Evaluation

A reusable rubric for scoring an AI-generated response before anyone relies on it. Each criterion is scored 0–2 with evidence — the same evidence-based scoring I used reviewing insurance claims at WNS, adapted for model output.

CriterionWhat I checkScale
AccuracyClaims are factually correct and grounded; nothing invented.0 · 1 · 2
CompletenessAnswers the whole request; no missing parts or steps.0 · 1 · 2
Instruction adherenceFollows format, scope, and constraints that were asked for.0 · 1 · 2
Tone & audience fitRegister matches the reader and the context.0 · 1 · 2
Safety & complianceNo sensitive-data leaks, unsafe advice, or policy breaches.0 · 1 · 2
Worked example
Prompt“Summarise this board paper in 5 bullets for the CFO.”
Output6 bullets, accurate, but one figure unverified and tone too casual.
ScoreAccuracy 2  Completeness 1  Tone 1
VerdictReturn for revision — flag the unverified figure, tighten to 5 bullets, lift register.
Sample 02
Operations Prompt Library
AI Workflows

Reusable prompts for recurring executive-operations tasks, each paired with a guardrail so the output stays safe to use. Built and refined through daily practice.

P-01

Executive brief from raw notes

Turn these notes into a 5-bullet brief for [role]. Lead with the decision needed. Mark any figure I haven't confirmed.
Guardrail · unverified data is flagged, never stated as fact.
P-02

Minutes → action tracker

From this transcript, extract decisions and actions as a table: owner · action · due. Omit discussion.
Guardrail · owners left blank if not explicitly named.
P-03

Document QA pass

Review this draft against the checklist below. Return only issues found, grouped by severity, with the line reference.
Guardrail · review only — no silent edits to the source.
